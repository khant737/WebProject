TRANSPORT SYSTEM IN PLANTS

1. Plants make their own organic molecules, using the process of photosynthesis.

2. Carbon dioxide is the source of carbon and light is the source of food.

3. The main photosynthetic organs are the plants.

4. Leaves have a large surface area to volume ratio.

5. Leaves have area for collection of light and distance for gases to diffuse in and out.

6. Most plants have compact bodies like animals.

7. Plants have extensive bodies with leaves below the ground.

8. Plants have extensive root systems below the ground.

9. Plant body spreads out to obtain the carbon dioxide, light energy, water and inorganic mineral ions from its soil.

10. Water and mineral ions are absorbed by roots and transported through the phloem to other plant parts.

11. Sugars are produced in leaves, but glucose is needed by all parts of the plant for photosynthesis.

12. Glucose can be moved through the phloem as part of the sucrose molecule.

13. Starch is usually changed into form of sugar, for storage in a potato tuber.
